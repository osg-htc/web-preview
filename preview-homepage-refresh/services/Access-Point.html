OSG Access Point

The OSG Access Point (AP) is a researcher's home for large-scale research computing. Researchers can place their workloads and data on the AP, automate workflows, and utilize global resources.

With a core powered by HTCSS, the AP helps the researcher manage their workload execution, allowing researchers to utilize resource pools like the Open Science Pool or the PATh facility. Researchers can run computational jobs, utilize GPUs, move data, and manage manage complex workflows.

Working with an AP requires no resources beyond your personal computer - it is your "home" on the nation's cyberinfrastructure, leading to our slogan of Submit Locally, Run Globally.

The power of the OSG AP comes from its ability to allow researchers to effectively harness their computing capacity. The AP provides tools to allow researchers to manage their jobs on a diverse set of resources, decreasing their time spent managing workloads, and increasing their computational throughput.

The OSG AP connects Cloud and Campus Resources, NSF awarded allocations and free to use resources such as OSPool, in one location accessible by researchers from their laptops.

OSG operates an AP available to any US-based researcher and their collaborators, OSGConnect.

Using OSGConnect, researchers can utilize resources such as the Open Science Pool and the Open Science Data Federation. These services provide computational resources and data capacity to execute your workloads.
